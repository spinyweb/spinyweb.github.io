Apple accused of contempt of court

  • Kiritu Ndekere
  • 1st May, 2025
  • 2 Comments

Apple recently suffered a major legal blow when a federal judge found the company in violation of a previous antitrust ruling regarding payment restrictions on the App Store. The case was even referred to federal prosecutors for criminal contempt of court, a case that the WSJ described as extremely rare.

"Apple has deliberately failed to comply with this court's injunction," Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers said, without hesitation . She even accused Apple of doing so with "the clear intention of creating new anti-competitive barriers. "

The focus of criticism was aimed squarely at senior leadership. Judge Rogers said that Apple CEO Tim Cook ignored advice from his longtime deputy Phil Schiller to comply with the ban. Instead, he chose to listen to his finance team.

“Cook made a bad choice,” the judge said. More seriously, she accused Apple’s vice president of finance, Alex Roman, of “blatantly lying under oath.”

The ruling is the latest development in a long-running legal battle between Apple and Epic Games, the developer of the popular game Fortnite, as the two sides argue over the 30% fee Apple collects from app developers.

Epic claims that Apple receives a whopping 78% profit from the app, which clearly shows Apple's monopolistic behavior.

In the 2021 case, Judge Rogers largely ruled in favor of Apple, but required the company to allow developers to offer other payment options outside the App Store.

However, Apple responded by requiring these developers to still pay a 27% fee on out-of-app transactions and putting up other hurdles.

These actions “defeat the purpose of the injunction and continue Apple’s anti-competitive behavior simply to maintain its revenue stream,” Rogers said.

WSJ quoted analysts as saying that Judge Rogers' tough tone and evidence in his ruling could increase legal pressure on Apple internationally.

This could end Apple's delays in complying with similar requirements in other regions, including a recent $565 million fine from the European Union (EU) for violating antitrust rules.
